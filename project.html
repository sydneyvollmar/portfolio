Trusted Travel Mobile App

UX Reserch & Design

My Project

In my course SI 482: Design Interaction Studio, I thoroughly researched and designed a new mobile application. I worked on a team of 4 other students, focusing on research while still helping work on critiquing wireframes.

My team's mobile application, Trusted Travel, came as a result of our research and problem statement. We noticed that most sources of recommendations for travel come through annonymous sources. With Trusted Travel, a user has the opportunity to connect with their friends and receive trustworthy recommendations.

Timeline: Fall 2019 Semester, 8 weeks

My Role: UX Researcher & Designer

My Course: Design Interaction Studio

My Takeaways

Throughout my time working on researching and designing a brand new mobile application, I had the chance to overcome obstacles facing my team and appreciate the hard work that comes with building up an idea from scratch. My team's problem statement that inspired our app's purpose in the first place served as more of a guide than I ever thought it would have, teaching me the importance of setting up your work with specific goals early on in order to have something to strive for.

My process during the creation of Trusted Travel included researching users, creating personas, creating low-fidelity wireframes, testing paper prototypes on potential end-users, creating high-fidelity wireframes on figma, and writing a report to sum up the process. The most rewarding step in the process was testing the lo-fi prototype on users, since it gave me real feedback that verified that our app was useful for our target age group. I hope to engage in this full research and design cycle again soon.
